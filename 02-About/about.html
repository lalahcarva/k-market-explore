ABOUT KENSINGTON MARKET



If you ask any resident of Toronto for a must-visit location in the city, they will undoubtedly recommend Kensington Market. Situated in downtown Toronto, Kensington Market encompasses an area bounded by College Street, Spadina Avenue, Dundas Street, and Bathurst Street. It showcases the visible remnants of Toronto's immigrant history intertwined with charming late-Victorian houses, creating a vibrant commercial neighborhood.

Kensington Market is not only a bustling vintage market but also a lively and vibrant community. It exists as a small, self-contained neighborhood within the larger city of Toronto. Most merchants in the market reside above their stores in the well-preserved Victorian homes dating back to the 1800s, which still grace the streets of this historic district. This enchanting district and marketplace span along Kensington and Augusta Avenues, north of Dundas Street, and extend to Baldwin Street, west of Spadina Avenue. Over the years, Kensington Market has been a welcoming haven for immigrants from diverse regions, shaping it into a thriving multicultural community since its inception in the 1800s. In recognition of its rich heritage and history, Kensington Market earned the official designation of a National Historic Site of Canada in 2006. Even today, Kensington Market continues to attract newcomers, including artists, musicians, and university students who frequent its bars and restaurants. The neighborhood boasts numerous cozy and budget-friendly eateries, known as "hole-in-the-wall" places, offering homemade dishes. Unsurprisingly, these establishments have become popular among students. Renowned as one of the oldest sections of Toronto, Kensington Market stands as an iconic market and a significant tourist destination. It gained further prominence as the backdrop for the television sitcom "King of Kensington," filmed from the mid-1970s to the 1980s, featuring Al Waxman as the beloved character Larry King. In honor of Waxman's contribution, Bellevue Square Park now showcases a statue of him. The park serves as a prominent venue for various concerts and festivals held throughout the summer season.
